KYC in 60 seconds. Replaces a four-to-six-month vendor stack.

Read the fields
Fields returned on every C2B, B2C, and Status response

confidence (0–1), device_match (bool), location_ok (bool), risk (enum: low, medium, high, blocked).

Rail-side inputs the score is grounded on

MSISDN registration age, SIM-swap window, device fingerprint, GSM Header Enrichment, location consistency against transaction history. Structurally unavailable to any card-based platform, pure fintech, or API aggregator.

Branches your code can take today

Personalize on cash rhythm without ever seeing a transaction — intelligence

Derived patterns, scope-limited consent. Raw transactions never cross the API.

Read the fields
Fields returned behind a scope-limited consent

rhythm (enum: weekly, bi-weekly, monthly, irregular), next_low (predicted date), categories[] (spend segments), churn_risk (0–1), spend_segment (decile).

Consent model

OAuth-style, scope-limited, time-boxed, revocable from the customer's phone. Downstream calls after revocation return consent: revoked — your app degrades gracefully without silent failure.

Data boundary

Derived patterns only. The transactions behind the rhythm never leave Safaricom. Your app gets the shape of the cash flow, not the movements that formed it.

Branches your code can take today

AI financial companions that warn before cash runs short. Pre-empty nudges without a raw-data pipeline. Credit terms priced on cash-flow shape rather than balance. Subscription audits on inferred categories.

Price credit to any of 3M+ merchants in real time — merchant_health

Categorical read computed on two-sided rail visibility. No credit bureau in the loop.

Read the fields
Categories returned on every B2C and C2B response

status (stable / growing / declining / at_risk), trend (MoM delta), risk_flag (null or categorical).

Factor indicators

Transaction velocity trend (rolling 30-day), days-active ratio, reversal rate, merchant-category cohort variance. Computed rail-side with two-sided transaction visibility no pure fintech or API aggregator can assemble.

Default availability

Attached to every B2C and C2B response by default. No separate endpoint. No per-query pricing. No consent flow — the signal describes the merchant you are already transacting with.

Branches your code can take today

Powered by identity

Disburse a microloan in 60 seconds.

A three-person fintech in Nairobi with no KYC team. Borrower enters their phone number, the app makes the collect() it was going to make anyway, reads identity.confidence and identity.risk on the response, approves KES 50,000 before the STK prompt expires.

Powered by intelligence

Warn a customer before cash runs short.

A mother in Malindi uses an AI financial companion. She grants consent once. The app reads intelligence.next_low and intelligence.rhythm, schedules a nudge for the day before her cash typically empties. She never exports a statement. The app never sees a transaction.

Retires: a raw-transaction scraping flow most customers refuse, and the open-banking or screen-scrape pipelines that used to be the only path to this signal. No raw history leaves the rail.

Powered by merchant_health

Extend same-day supplier credit.

A merchant in Eldoret opens her till for the first time. A wholesaler reads merchant_health.status on the B2B settlement they already run against her shortcode. growing triggers 30-day terms; at_risk triggers cash-on-delivery. No application form. No credit check. Same-day credit.

Retires: a credit bureau subscription and an in-house merchant-underwriting model refreshed monthly. The signal is live, two-sided, and computed on data the bureau never sees.

Powered by identity + merchant_health

Score a P2P transfer for fraud before the PIN entry.

A remittance app for the Nairobi–Kampala corridor reads identity.risk on the sender and merchant_health.risk_flag on the recipient merchant before the STK prompt fires. High-risk pairs gate to a step-up flow; low-risk pairs ship straight to the PIN.

Retires: a standalone fraud engine. Structurally unavailable on card networks — this pattern depends on MSISDN-level telco signal fused with two-sided merchant visibility.

DATA STAYS ON THE RAIL

Your code gets the decision. Safaricom keeps the raw.

Every field on this page is derived at rail level. Your app receives what it needs to branch — a confidence score, a category, a predicted date — not the data behind it. The customer's trust in M-Pesa stays intact. Your product gets to build on it.

  • Derived signals only intelligence.rhythm is a pattern, not the transactions that formed it. merchant_health.status is a category, not the velocity trend behind it. Raw history never leaves Safaricom.
  • Consent where it matters intelligence requires OAuth-style, scope-limited, revocable consent granted from the customer's phone. identity and merchant_health attach to transactions you are already authorized to process — no additional consent surface required.
  • Revocation is immediate After a customer revokes, the very next call returns consent: revoked. Your app degrades gracefully; no silent failure, no stale signal.
  • PII sanitization on the SDK Phone numbers masked by default (254708374149 becomes 254708***149) in logs, MCP tool outputs, and anything that might enter an AI agent context.
  • Reconcilable audit Every intelligence field on a response traces back to the rail call that produced it. You ship the decision. Safaricom holds the receipt.
